#### 4.3.1 Purpose of the Event

The event was organized to provide Swinburne Vietnam students with the opportunity to gain hands-on exposure to the real working environment in the Cloud Computing field, helping them better understand how businesses today build, operate, and develop application systems on the Cloud platform. Additionally, the program served as a bridge for students to network directly with industry professionals through the **AWS First Cloud AI Journey** community, creating opportunities to learn practical experience, orient their careers, and stay updated with the latest technology trends.

#### 4.3.3 Notable Content

##### Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung – Market Overview and Career Trends in Cloud Computing

He shared an overview of the current IT market landscape and analyzed major shifts in enterprise hiring demands.

* The IT job market is becoming increasingly competitive. Even for intern positions, companies set relatively high professional requirements, typically the ability to work with Kubernetes (K8S) and modern technologies.
* Cloud Computing has become an inevitable development trend in the technology industry. The Cloud market scale has now far exceeded traditional hardware, and most large enterprises prioritize a **Cloud First** strategy when designing and deploying systems.
* The strong growth of AI has also changed workforce requirements. Young engineers need to continuously improve their professional competence and ability to solve complex problems to meet increasingly high enterprise expectations.

##### Mr. Vinh Banh – Differences Between Academic and Real Work Environments

Through practical experience in Data Engineering, he highlighted many differences between learning at university and working at enterprises.

* In academic environments, data is often pre-standardized, problem requirements are clear, and students have more time to complete tasks. In contrast, real-world environments require processing data from multiple sources, with constantly changing business requirements and significant time pressure.
* Learning should focus on understanding the essence of frameworks and operating principles rather than just memorizing how to use tools.
* AI will become a supporting tool to increase work productivity but cannot completely replace humans. Business domain understanding, analytical thinking, and communication skills remain differentiating factors that AI can hardly replace.

##### Ms. Nhu Tran – Overcoming Fear and Proactively Seizing Opportunities

The talk focused on essential soft skills for students to adapt and thrive in the corporate environment.

* Students need to learn to overcome the fear of making mistakes and the pressure of being judged by others to boldly try new opportunities.
* Communication skills play a crucial role in work, helping to avoid unnecessary misunderstandings and improve collaboration effectiveness among team members.
* Proactively building presence through short conversations or daily exchanges helps build better relationships with colleagues and superiors.

##### Mr. Khang Nguyen – Skills, Mindset, and Pressure from AI Development

The talk emphasized the role of learning mindset and work attitude in the context of rapidly advancing AI.

* AI is a useful tool for students to learn and solve problems more effectively; however, they should not become completely dependent on AI but should instead train their analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities.
* The most important thing is to understand the essence of knowledge rather than just focusing on the output results.
* For Fresher positions, employers typically evaluate candidates based on their learning attitude and growth mindset first, then professional competence and practical experience.

#### 4.3.4 Lessons Learned

After participating in the event, I gained many valuable lessons not only in professional knowledge but also in future career development direction.

* **Proactively build personal presence:** Besides improving professional skills, students should actively participate in technology communities, expand their network, and proactively connect with industry professionals to create more learning and development opportunities.
* **Invest in long-term value:** Continuously learning, updating new technologies, and maintaining a lifelong learning mindset will enhance competitiveness and create advantages when entering the labor market.
* **Broaden business perspective:** When orienting careers, one should not only focus on technical knowledge but also equip themselves with an understanding of the business characteristics of each field, enabling them to build technology solutions that meet real enterprise needs.
